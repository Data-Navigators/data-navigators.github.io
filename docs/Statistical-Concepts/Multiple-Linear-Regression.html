  3. Age: Negative coefficient (-0.7988), but it is not statistically significant (p-value > 0.05), suggesting that age might not play a significant role in predicting the total amount.
  4. Gender: Both male and female coefficients are negative, with males contributing -104.99 and females contributing -116.16 to the total amount. These coefficients are statistically significant (p-value < 0.05), indicating that gender has an impact on the total amount.

The R-squared value of 0.855 indicates that 85.5% of the variance in Total Amount is explained by the model. This is a good fit, meaning the model explains a large portion of the data's variability.

Q2: How do we interpret the regression coefficients?â€‹

ðŸ’¡ View Answer
  1. Quantity (177.4215): For each additional unit sold, the Total Amount increases by 177.42. This is a strong and significant positive effect.
  2. Price per Unit (2.4941): For every 1-unit increase in the price of a product, the Total Amount increases by 2.49. This indicates that higher-priced products generate more revenue.
  3. Age (-0.7988): Age has a small negative impact, but this coefficient is not statistically significant (p-value > 0.05), meaning age doesnâ€™t significantly influence the Total Amount.
  4. Gender_Male (-104.9903): Being male results in a reduction of 104.99 in Total Amount compared to the baseline (which might be non-binary or another gender category).
  5. Gender_Female (-116.1623): Similarly, being female results in a reduction of 116.16 in Total Amount.
  6. Product Categories (Clothing, Electronics, Beauty): All have significant negative coefficients, meaning that when compared to a reference product category (likely another omitted one like Food or Household items), these categories result in a lower Total Amount.

The negative coefficients for gender and product categories suggest that these groups spend less or purchase fewer high-ticket items compared to the reference groups.

The Adjusted R-Squared (0.854) takes into account the number of predictors in the model and adjusts for overfitting. It tells us that 85.4% of the variance in Total Amount is explained by the independent variables (Quantity, Price per Unit, Age, Gender, Product Category), while also penalizing the addition of non-significant predictors.

This is a high Adjusted R-Squared value, indicating that the model fits the data well without being overly complex.
